Is Interstellar the best sci-fi movie of the last decade?

Movies

After rewatching Interstellar for the fifth time last night, I'm more convinced than ever that it might be the greatest science fiction movie of the 2010s. The blend of hard science, emotional storytelling, and breathtaking visuals creates something truly special.

The movie tackles complex concepts like relativity, black holes, and the nature of time in a way that's accessible without dumbing things down too much. Hans Zimmer's score perfectly complements the vast emptiness of space while still feeling intimate during the family moments.

What do you all think? Does Interstellar deserve to be considered the best sci-fi film of the last decade, or are there other contenders that surpass it? I'm especially interested in hearing from those who might rank Arrival, Ex Machina, or Blade Runner 2049 above it.

Interstellar's representation of a black hole was groundbreaking! The VFX team actually worked with physicist Kip Thorne to create the most scientifically accurate depiction ever put to film.
